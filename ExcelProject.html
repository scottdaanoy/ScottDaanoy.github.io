Data Analyst Project: Bike Sales

Overview

In this project, the goal is to create a dashboard for bike sales that will answer the following questions:

  • Show information about customers that bought bikes
  • Show statistics about customers that bought bikes

Throughout the project, the following skills and techniques were demonstrated:

  • Data Cleansing
  • Data Wrangling
  • Data Visualisation
  • Descriptive Analytics
  • Excel

Data Cleansing & Transformation

In Excel, I started the data preparation phase by cleansing and transforming the data. This step is a crucial part of the process as it can ensure the accuracy and reliability of visualisations and analytics. Below are the following steps I took during this process:

  • Assess the data quality by identifying and addressing any anomalies, duplicates, inconsistencies or missing values
  • Use techniques in Excel to cleanse the data
  • Create pivot tables to select data used for visualisations

Dashboard

The final dashboard includes the following features:

  • A visualisation showing the average income of each gender and if they bought a bike
  • A visualisation showing the number of people in each age bracket and if they bought a bike
  • A visualisation showing the number of people in each commute distance bracket and if they bought a bike
  • Filters for: marital status, region and education history

Conclusions

After visualising the data, descriptive analytics can be performed to identify the following conclusions:

  • Middle-aged people are more likely to buy a bike
  • Males are more likely to buy a bike than females
  • The longer the commute is for a customer, they are less likely to buy a bike

Using the information from this dasboard, we can now answer the following questions given the data:

  • Which customers are more likely to buy a bike
  • Why are customers more likely to buy a bike
  • Predict whether or not someone will buy a bike
